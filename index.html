The sample conclusion

The Shopify returns category is not empty, but the first wedge should not be another full returns platform. AfterShip, Loop, Return Prime, and ReturnGO already cover portals, labels, exchanges, rules, reporting, and store credit. The useful signal is where merchants still hit repeated friction.

Observed friction Why it matters Possible wedge
Plan limits discovered late Merchants can spend time configuring before realizing the workflow needs a higher plan. Pre-launch plan and feature checklist.
Label, carrier, and multi-item edge cases Small setup errors can turn into support tickets. Return label setup review.
Returns, exchanges, store credit, and reporting are tangled Revenue retention depends on the details, not just the portal. Returns vs exchanges reporting template.
Low-volume merchants are price-sensitive A $100+/month platform may not fit stores with low return volume. Lightweight native Shopify plus simple process guide.
Markets, multi-currency, and domain complexity Narrower, higher-friction setups may justify custom review. Compatibility audit for advanced Shopify stores.

What a paid audit includes

This is not a broad market report. It is a pre-build decision artifact: what buyers complain about, which complaints repeat across competitors, what substitutes already exist, and what a 7-day validation should test.

Competitor snapshot

3-5 competitors, public reviews, pricing, positioning, visible substitutes, and trust signals.

Complaint buckets

Low-star reviews grouped by price, setup, support, feature boundaries, integrations, reporting, or workflow risk.

Decision labels

Proceed, Narrow, Hold, or Reject, with the reason and a smallest validation action.
